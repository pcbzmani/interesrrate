Asaktivatti User Guide

A complete guide to managing informal and semi-formal loans — with full support for Indian paise-per-rupee interest rates.

What is Asaktivatti?

Asaktivatti is a personal lending management app designed for people who lend money to friends, family, or business associates and want to track it properly — without complicated bank software.

Google Sheets Setup

1
Open Google Sheets (sheets.google.com) and create a new blank spreadsheet. Give it a name like "Asaktivatti Data".
2
Click Extensions → Apps Script in the top menu. A new script editor tab opens.
3
Delete everything in the editor. Copy all the contents of Code.gs from the Asaktivatti repository and paste it in.
4
Click Deploy → New Deployment. Set:
Type: Web App · Execute as: Me · Who can access: Anyone
Click Deploy and authorize when prompted.
5
Copy the Web App URL (looks like https://script.google.com/macros/s/…/exec).
6
In Asaktivatti, go to ⚙️ Settings, paste the URL, and click Save & Connect.
Tip: The sheets are created automatically on first use — you don't need to create any columns manually. Three sheets appear: Borrowers, Transactions, Transfers.
Security: Your Web App URL is your API key. Anyone with this URL can read and write your data. Keep it private. If compromised, delete the deployment and create a new one.

Interest Rate Input Types

Asaktivatti supports four ways to enter interest rates. All are equivalent — just different ways people express the same thing. The app converts automatically.

Input TypeUnitWho uses itExample
% per annum Annual percentage Banks, formal lenders, loan documents "12% p.a."
% per month Monthly percentage Some informal lenders, credit cards "1% per month"
Paise / ₹ / month Paise per rupee per month Most common in India — village lenders, chit funds, friends/family "2 paise" or "do paise"
₹ per ₹100 / month Rupees per hundred per month Another Indian expression, same as paise "₹2 per ₹100 per month"

Understanding Paise per Rupee

This is the most common way interest rates are expressed in informal lending across India — particularly in rural areas, small towns, and among non-banking communities.

The concept: "1 paise per rupee per month" means for every ₹1 you lend, the borrower pays back 1 paise (₹0.01) per month as interest. This equals 1% per month = 12% per annum.

How the math works

Interest = Principal × (Paise ÷ 100) × Months Example: ₹50,000 at 2 paise for 12 months Simple Interest = 50,000 × (2 ÷ 100) × 12 = ₹12,000

Loan Type: EMI (Reducing Balance)

Best for: Standard loans where you want equal monthly payments. Most common and fair to the borrower.

Equal monthly installments. Each EMI pays off some principal and some interest. As principal reduces, interest portion decreases each month.

EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ − 1) where: P = principal, r = monthly decimal rate, n = months

Example: ₹1,00,000 at 2 paise (24% p.a.) for 24 months → EMI = ₹5,289/month, Total interest = ₹26,938

Loan Type: Simple Interest

Best for: Short-term loans, informal agreements, easy mental calculation.
Total Interest = Principal × Monthly Rate × Months Monthly Payment = (Principal + Total Interest) ÷ Months

Example: ₹50,000 at 2 paise for 6 months → Interest = ₹50,000 × 2% × 6 = ₹6,000. Monthly = ₹9,333

Loan Type: Flat Rate

Note: Flat rate appears cheaper than EMI but has a higher effective rate. 1 paise flat ≠ 1 paise EMI.
Monthly Payment = (Principal ÷ Months) + (Principal × Monthly Rate) Interest is always on the original principal, not the reducing balance

Loan Type: Interest Only (Bullet)

Best for: Business loans, bridge financing, seasonal lending where the borrower has irregular cash flows.

Borrower pays only interest every month. Full principal is returned at the end of the term. Useful when the borrower needs cash flow flexibility.

Monthly Payment = Principal × Monthly Rate Final Month = Monthly Interest + Full Principal

Recording Payments

When a borrower pays you, record it in two ways:

Quick Pay (from Borrowers list or Dashboard)

Click the green 💳 Pay button on any borrower card. A modal appears with pre-filled EMI amount and quick buttons for Full, Half, or EMI amounts.

From Borrower Profile

Open the borrower's profile → click 💳 Pay button at the top. Same modal, but you can also see the full transaction history below.

Partial payments are fine. Enter any amount — the outstanding balance updates accordingly. If the full balance is paid off, the loan is automatically marked as Closed.

Pre-payments

If a borrower pays ahead of schedule, simply record the payment. The outstanding balance decreases. The payment schedule shows historical vs future payments.

Borrower-to-Borrower Transfers

This handles a real-world situation unique to informal lending: one borrower repays through another.

Scenario: Debt Chain

You lent ₹1 lakh to Ramesh and ₹50,000 to Priya. Ramesh calls and says "I've given ₹20,000 to Priya directly on your behalf."

In Asaktivatti: Go to Transfers → From: Ramesh → To: Priya → Amount: ₹20,000

Result: Ramesh's debt reduces by ₹20,000. Priya's debt increases by ₹20,000. Both transaction histories are updated.

Scenario: Guarantor Takes Over Debt

Anil borrowed ₹75,000 but can no longer repay. His guarantor Suresh agrees to take over the debt.

In Asaktivatti: Transfers → From: Anil → To: Suresh → Amount: ₹75,000 (Anil's outstanding)

Result: Anil's balance becomes ₹0 (loan closes). Suresh's outstanding increases by ₹75,000.

Transfers cannot be reversed. If you make a mistake, record a reverse transfer to undo it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I change the interest rate after creating a loan?

Yes — go to the borrower's profile → Edit. The rate change affects future calculations and the displayed schedule, but does not retroactively change past transactions.

What happens when a borrower is marked Overdue?

Loans past their scheduled end date with a remaining balance are automatically flagged as Overdue (shown with a red badge). You can continue recording payments normally — the loan stays open until fully paid.

Can I delete a transaction?

Not directly (to preserve audit trail). To reverse a payment, record a Disbursement of the same amount with a note like "Payment reversal — entered by mistake".

Is my data secure?

Data is stored in your personal Google Sheets, accessible only via your Apps Script URL. Don't share the URL. The app doesn't send data anywhere else.

Can I run this offline?

The app itself (index.html) can run from a local file. But it needs internet to reach Google Sheets. In Demo Mode (no URL), it works fully offline with in-memory storage.

What's the difference between "2 paise flat" and "2 paise EMI"?

With Flat Rate, interest is always on the full original principal. With EMI (reducing balance), interest is on the remaining balance which decreases over time. For the same paise rate, Flat Rate costs more than EMI.

Troubleshooting

ProblemSolution
Connection test fails Re-check the URL (copy fresh from Apps Script deployment page). Make sure you deployed with "Anyone" access, not "Anyone with Google Account".
Data shows in app but not in Sheet You may be in Demo Mode. Check Settings — if connected, the green ✅ indicator shows in the sidebar.
Apps Script authorization error In the Apps Script editor, run any function manually (e.g., doGet) to trigger the authorization flow, then deploy again.
Sheet shows old data after updating Code.gs After editing Code.gs, you must create a New Deployment (not redeploy the same version) to see changes.
App is slow / timeout on first load Google Apps Script has a cold start delay (~2–5 seconds). Subsequent requests are faster. This is a free-tier limitation.
CORS error in browser console Make sure the deployment access is set to "Anyone" (not "Anyone with Google Account") and you're using the correct exec URL ending in /exec.
